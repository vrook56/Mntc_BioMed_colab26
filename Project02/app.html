🔬 FORENSIC BACKGROUND — FINGERPRINT ANALYSIS

Fingerprint analysis is one of the oldest and most reliable forensic techniques. Every person has unique ridge patterns on their fingertips — composed of loops, whorls, and arches — that remain unchanged throughout life. Forensic examiners use a 12-point matching system to compare latent prints (found at scenes) with known prints (from suspects).

🔁
Loop patterns — most common (~65%), ridges enter and exit from the same side
🌀
Whorl patterns — (~30%), ridges form circular or spiral patterns
⌒
Arch patterns — least common (~5%), ridges enter from one side and exit the other

🔬 FORENSIC BACKGROUND — DNA SEQUENCING

DNA profiling (also called DNA fingerprinting) analyzes Short Tandem Repeats (STRs) — specific locations in the genome where short sequences repeat. Since everyone (except identical twins) has unique STR patterns, DNA evidence can identify individuals with near-certainty. The FBI's CODIS system uses 20 STR loci.

🔬 FORENSIC BACKGROUND — BLOOD ANALYSIS

Forensic blood analysis encompasses blood typing (ABO + Rh system), DNA extraction from white blood cells, and spatter pattern analysis. Blood type can narrow suspects while DNA from blood provides definitive identification. The ABO system has 4 types: A, B, AB, and O. Roughly 44% of the population is Type O.

🔬 FORENSIC BACKGROUND — TRACE EVIDENCE

Trace evidence refers to small, often microscopic materials transferred between a criminal and a crime scene — following Locard's Exchange Principle: "Every contact leaves a trace." Examples include hair fibers, shoe tread analysis, glass fragments, soil samples, and chemical residues. Even a single fiber can link a suspect to a scene.

🔬 FORENSIC SCIENCE TAKEAWAYS
👆
Fingerprint Analysis — Ridge patterns are unique and permanent. Used since the 1890s, still one of the most powerful forensic tools.
🧬
DNA Profiling — Developed in 1984 by Sir Alec Jeffreys. STR analysis can identify individuals with 1-in-quintillion probability of false match.
🩸
Blood Typing — ABO system discovered by Karl Landsteiner in 1901. Blood type alone can't identify an individual but can eliminate suspects.
👣
Locard's Exchange Principle — "Every contact leaves a trace." Every criminal brings something to and takes something from the scene.
